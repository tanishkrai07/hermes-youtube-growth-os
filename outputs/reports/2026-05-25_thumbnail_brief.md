## 📋 THUMBNAIL DESIGN RULES (Dr. Kane Standard)

Every thumbnail follows the **3-Element Rule**:
1. **Doctor Face** — Dr. Kane's expressive face, shot from shoulders up, wearing white coat or professional attire. Emotion must match the hook (concerned, shocked, authoritative, or reassuring).
2. **Bold Text** — Maximum 5-6 words. High-contrast, sans-serif font (Impact or Montserrat Bold). Text must be readable at mobile thumbnail size (160×90px).
3. **Visual Trigger** — A prop, graphic element, or background that creates curiosity or emotional response.

**Color Palette Standards:**
- Primary: Deep navy blue (#1a237e) — trust, medical authority
- Accent: Bright red (#d32f2f) — urgency, danger, STOP
- Accent: Bright yellow (#fdd835) — attention, highlight
- Background: Dark gradient or clinical setting
- Text: White (#ffffff) with black stroke/shadow for readability

**Layout Zones:**
- **Left 40%:** Dr. Kane's face (close-up, expressive)
- **Right 60%:** Bold text + visual trigger
- **Top/Bottom bars:** Optional accent stripes for brand consistency

**What We NEVER Do:**
- No misleading imagery
- No fearmongering without scientific basis
- No cluttered compositions (max 3 elements)
- No text smaller than readable at mobile size
- No copying competitor thumbnails
