- **Right third:** Visual trigger — Dr. Kane's wrist with fingers on it. Radiating from the wrist: a pulse waveform that transitions from regular (left side, steady peaks) to irregular (right side, chaotic/AFib-like pattern). The waveform glows subtly — normal in blue-white, irregular in red-tinged.

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Deep teal (#004D40) — distinctive, medical but warm, distinct from all previous navy/indigo/burgundy palettes

**Emotional Trigger:** Authority + Curious Urgency. The viewer feels: "A doctor is showing me something about my heartbeat I didn't know." The pulse waveform split (regular → irregular) creates immediate visual intrigue at any size. The gesture of checking his own pulse is both authoritative and relatable.

## Production Notes

### For Both Videos
- **Resolution:** 1280×720px (minimum for YouTube)
- **File format:** PNG or high-quality JPEG (<2MB)
- **Safe zone:** Keep critical text/face within 1200×630px center (mobile crop)
- **Font:** Montserrat Bold/Extra Bold/Regular (Google Fonts, free)
- **Doctor photo:** Use consistent Dr. Kane headshot library. White coat, clean background, studio lighting. Expression varies per variant.
- **Lab report prop:** For VID-022, use a realistic lab report prop with actual formatting (don't fake specific values that look unrealistic)

### Color Accessibility
- All text passes WCAG AA contrast ratio on dark backgrounds
- Color-blind safe: teal/cyan and forest green/amber are distinguishable in deuteranopia/protanopia simulations
- Waveform in VID-021 uses both color AND pattern (regular vs irregular) to convey meaning
- Split panels in VID-022 use symbols (X, ⚠, arrows) in addition to color
