# Dr. Victor Kane First 10 Long-Form Launch Slate

Purpose: first 10 long-form videos for the 90-day push to 100K subscribers.
Standard length: 14-16 minutes per long-form video, target 15 minutes, 2,400-2,800 scripted words.
Short strategy: extract one 45-60 second Short from each long-form video for the following upload day.

## Operating Rules

- Use the name Dr. Victor Kane in every script, title, description, thumbnail brief, and metadata package.
- Keep every public title under 70 characters and include an age marker.
- Persona A: Cardiothoracic Surgeon, urgent and dramatic. Use for medication, heart, stroke, blood pressure, surgery, and hard warning topics.
- Persona B: Senior Health and Vascular Medicine Specialist, warm and explanatory. Use for food, sleep, lifestyle, legs, muscle, pain, brain, and daily habit topics.
- Every video must follow Fear -> Trust -> Hope -> Action.
- Never leave the viewer in fear. Every video closes with a free or low-cost action and a specific question to ask a doctor when medical decisions are involved.
- Medication topics must not tell viewers to stop prescriptions. Use: "Talk to your doctor and specifically ask..."
- Every video launches with two thumbnails: Version A red/yellow fear and Version B dark/navy authority.

## Video 1

Title: Dr. Victor Kane: NEVER Reheat These 5 Foods After 60
Pillar: Food + Warning
Persona: B, Senior Health and Vascular Medicine Specialist
Score: 18/20
Target length: 15 minutes

Core idea: Start with a familiar kitchen habit, then reveal how reheating and storage mistakes can increase food-safety risk, inflammation, blood sugar stress, or digestive strain in older adults.

Why this goes first: Reheated-food warning videos performed strongly in the transcript set, and this topic combines fear, familiarity, and immediate action.

List spine:
1. Reheated rice: explain storage temperature, bacterial growth risk, and the safe cooling rule.
2. Reheated cooking oils: explain oxidation, repeated frying, and why old oil is different from fresh oil.
3. Reheated processed meats: explain sodium, nitrates, and heart strain without making unsupported cancer claims.
4. Reheated leafy greens: explain nitrate conversion risk and why storage matters more than the vegetable itself.
5. Reheated potatoes: explain resistant starch benefits versus unsafe storage and blood sugar swings.

Hope close: Cool leftovers quickly, refrigerate within 2 hours, reheat once, and discard anything with smell, slime, or uncertain storage history.

Thumbnail A: Red background, doctor right, text left: "STOP REHEATING THIS", prop: microwave plate with red X.
Thumbnail B: Dark authority background, doctor pointing, text: "SENIORS BEWARE", prop: food container with warning icon.

Short hook: "STOP reheating this one leftover after 60 until you know how it was stored."
