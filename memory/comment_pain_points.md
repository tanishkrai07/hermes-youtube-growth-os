# Comment Pain Points & Audience Psychology

Last updated: 2026-05-16

## Top Pain Points

1. **Fear of sudden death** — Stroke, heart attack, virus = highest engagement
2. **Confusion about symptoms** — "Is this normal or dangerous?" = high comment activity
3. **Distrust of doctors** — "What your doctor won't tell you" = strong CTR
4. **Food anxiety** — "Is my food killing me?" = evergreen demand
5. **Body signal confusion** — Nails, legs, ankles, feet = curiosity gap
6. **Longevity desire** — "Live 30 more years" = aspirational hook
7. **Medication concerns** — "Is my prescription hurting me?" = high trust content

## Emotional Triggers (ranked by engagement)

1. Fear of the unknown — "You don't know this is happening inside you"
2. Survival instinct — "This could save your life"
3. Curiosity gap — "The #3 item is the one nobody suspects"
4. Authority trust — "A cardiologist with 30 years reveals..."
5. Urgency — "24 hours before" / "right now in your body"
6. Hope — "Simple fix" / "one morning habit"

## Comment Themes

- "I have this symptom, what should I do?"
- "My doctor never told me this"
- "Thank you Dr. [Name], you saved my life"
- "I'm showing this to my husband/wife"
- "Can you make a video about [specific condition]?"

## Audience Psychology Insights

### Primary Fear: Invisible Internal Threats
The 60-80 audience is most afraid of things happening INSIDE their body that they can't see or feel until it's too late. This is why "Your Body Is Hiding X" and "X Hours Before Disaster" formats work so well.

### Secondary Fear: Being Misled by Doctors
There's a deep distrust of the medical system. Content that reveals "what your doctor won't tell you" or "the number doctors get wrong" performs exceptionally well.

### Tertiary Desire: Simple Solutions
After fear comes hope. The audience wants ONE simple action — a morning habit, a food to avoid, a vitamin to take. Not complex protocols.

### Content Consumption Pattern
- Watch time peaks in first 30 seconds (hook critical)
- Drop-off at 2-3 minutes if no list promise
- Re-engagement at each numbered item
- Final CTA must be simple and free (subscribe, share with spouse)
